Army Center Targeting: Current Execution Flow

How AiOrchestrator::order() weights MilitaryBrain center-pair pressures by nearby source centers, then issues army commands directly toward the selected influence-map center.

Two Decisions

POSITIONALWhich objective wins for this unit?

Every army unit is evaluated independently. A pressure only contributes when its source center is nearby. The highest accumulated target score selects the unit's objective bucket.

DIRECT CENTERWhere does the selected bucket go?

The bucket goes directly to the center it selected. There is no second brain, weighted map aggregation, candidate list, or shared areas[0] target.

Fog of war is intentionally ignored: enemy and battle centers remain valid targets even when stale or hidden. If a center does not exist, units assigned to it enter the hold path.

Reading a Center-Pair Pressure

For every directional pair source -> target, the calculator measures only the unit's distance to source. It multiplies that pair's pressure by clamp(1 - distanceToSource / 120, 0, 1) and adds the result to the target's score. It then divides each target score by the six possible source contributions, normalizing it to [0, 1]. The distance to target does not reduce the score.

Consequently, a unit at its own army center can receive full OUR_ARMY -> ENEMY_ARMY pressure even when the enemy army center is far away. Conversely, a unit farther than 120 units from every available source center receives no pressure and follows the low-pressure hold path.

Commands and Edge Cases

FARGo to the center

If squared distance to the selected center exceeds 30 * 30, the unit receives GO(targetCenter). Its normalized pressure sets a command-priority lock at pressure * 4.5.

CLOSEAttack locally

When within 30 units of the selected center, the unit does not queue a new move. It attacks the closest nearby enemy if one exists.

LOW PRESSUREHold

If the best calculated score is <= 0.1, including when every pressure source is more than 120 units away, the unit enters the hold group. Free hold units can still attack a nearby enemy.

MISSING CENTERHold

If the selected influence center is unavailable, every unit in that bucket enters the hold group. There is no alternate heatmap target or center fallback.

Command priority guard

A new command is only queued when its normalized pressure exceeds the unit's current command priority. Submitted commands set priority to pressure * 4.5; at the start of each order(), it is multiplied by 0.9. This prevents weaker new objectives from replacing a stronger current command.
